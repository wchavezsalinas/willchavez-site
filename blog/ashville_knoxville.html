Asheville/Knoxville

April 9, 2018

Towards the end of March, my team at IBM was given Thu+Fri off and during the beginning of that week I started looking into a strange little festival that I heard of, called Big Ears. It had been a little more than a month before my last travel (Atlanta) and I was itching to go somewhere. I saw that Four Tet was going to be there and I was going through a phase of just listening to his music on repeat.

Like an idiot I scheduled two nights in Knoxville, unknowingly realizing later that I'd spend all day Friday in Asheville. Asheville is that one city in NC that everyone loves, and I had never been to. I went full planning-mode and scheduled a tour of the Moog Music Factory, and a tour of the New Belgium Brewery (I know that doesn't sound like a lot of plannig but for me it is shiiiii).

So Moog Music is an amazing company that creates beautiful synthesizers. The founder Robert Moog created the first modular synth, and the rest was history. It felt so inspiring to be there and to play all the available synthesizers they have. As a musician, I find the application of LFA filters/envelops/oscillators to be heaven. Anything that really lets me modulate the sound to my liking fills me with so much happiness. The tour was great, they first gave a brief overview of the history of some of the instruments, like the theremin, and the mini-moog, the moog-55. Later came the tour of the production line, oh yeah I didn't mention, all of their analog synthesizers are made RIGHT THERE in Asheville. I saw tons of engineers with oscilloscops, voltage meters, soldering irons left and right. It really took me back to my days as a computer engineering major in college.
